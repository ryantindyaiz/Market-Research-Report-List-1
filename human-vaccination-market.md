<p><p>The human vaccination market comprises various types of vaccines. Inactivated vaccines contain killed pathogens, provoking an immune response without causing disease. Live-attenuated vaccines use weakened forms of pathogens for stronger immunity. mRNA vaccines employ synthetic genetic material to instruct cells to produce an antigen, eliciting an immune response. Conjugate vaccines link polysaccharides from pathogens to proteins to enhance immunogenicity. Other vaccines include viral vector and protein subunit types, each tailored for specific diseases, enhancing overall public health.</p></p>
