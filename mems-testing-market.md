<p><p>The MEMS testing market encompasses various techniques, with Kelvin Testing and Four-Probe Testing being prominent types. Kelvin Testing, known for its precision, is used to measure low resistance, ensuring accurate assessments of MEMS components' electrical performance. In contrast, Four-Probe Testing employs four separate probes to minimize contact resistance, allowing for precise characterization of the material properties of MEMS devices. Both methods are crucial for ensuring quality and reliability in the rapidly evolving MEMS industry.</p></p>
