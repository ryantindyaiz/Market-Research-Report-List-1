<p><p>The Novel Drug Reconstitution System market is categorized into bottled and bagged segments. Bottled systems typically involve prefilled vials containing lyophilized drugs that require reconstitution with a diluent before administration. This method ensures stability and extended shelf life. In contrast, bagged systems utilize flexible bags to store liquid formulations, allowing for easier manipulation and enhanced delivery of medications. Both types offer benefits in handling drug stability, administration efficiency, and patient compliance in various healthcare settings.</p></p>
