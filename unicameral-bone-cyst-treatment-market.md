<p><p>The treatment market for unicameral bone cysts comprises three main types: nonoperative treatment, curettage, and grafting. Nonoperative treatment typically involves observation or monitoring, allowing for potential spontaneous resolution of the cyst. Curettage involves surgically scraping out the cyst to remove its debris, often combined with a grafting procedure. Grafting replaces the cystic area with bone or bone-like materials to promote healing and structural integrity. These approaches cater to varying patient needs based on cyst size, location, and symptoms.</p></p>
